My Story
01

From metallurgy
to membranes
to multiscale
modeling.

I began as a metallurgical engineer at Mehran University (Pakistan), learning phase diagrams, heat treatment, and failure analysis. My undergraduate thesis investigated aluminium-alloy weld optimization—predicting microhardness from aging parameters using Bayesian machine learning (15% error reduction vs. empirical models).

After graduation, I joined SABIC (Riyadh, Saudi Arabia) as a materials testing trainee. I conducted 100+ SEM/EDS analyses on failed industrial components, reducing unclassified failure modes by 30% through systematic microstructural diagnostics. That year taught me that the hardest materials problems aren't in textbooks—they're in production lines where seconds of downtime cost thousands of dollars.

"The gap between what we can engineer in a lab and what people can actually afford, maintain, and rely on—that's the problem worth solving."

In 2018, I co-founded an off-grid solar + battery startup in rural Sindh, Pakistan (flood-affected region). We deployed 15+ systems for 200+ residents with no grid access. The technical challenge—designing 24-hour battery autonomy in 50°C heat with no maintenance infrastructure—forced me to confront energy storage's central paradox: it's too expensive, not durable enough, and not reliable enough for the people who need it most.

That failure drove me to NUST MISIS (Moscow), where I won an Open Door Scholarship (top 5% globally) for an MSc in Advanced Materials. My thesis: Fabrication, Optimization, and Theoretical Structure Studies of PVA-Based Electrospun Fibrous Membranes for Ion Conduction Applications.

The work combines experimental nanofabrication (electrospinning 50+ membrane samples), electrochemical validation (EIS: 41.4 mS/cm conductivity), and multiscale computational modeling:

  • 23 DFT calculations (ORCA: B3LYP-D3BJ, ωB97X-D3) quantifying cooperative H₃PO₄ binding (−203 kJ/mol), covalent phosphorylation stability (+251.9 kJ/mol), and Grotthuss proton-transfer barriers (19.5 kJ/mol)
  • 500 ps MD simulations (LAMMPS, OPLS-AA) showing acid immobilization: P–O RDF peak stable at 4.25 Å, MSD <3 Å² (vs. 18,000 Å² for free diffusion)
  • COMSOL fuel-cell modeling validating the full multiscale chain: model resistance 0.278 Ω vs. experimental EIS 0.276 Ω (0.7% error)

Alongside my thesis, I interned at Skoltech's Center for Digital Engineering, applying Python and MATLAB to ultrasonic signal processing for railway infrastructure monitoring (ML pattern recognition: 35% false-positive reduction). Different domain, same principle: measure carefully, model rigorously, validate relentlessly.

Available from September 2026. Seeking PhD or R&D roles in electrochemical materials, membrane engineering, or energy-storage systems where computational predictions meet experimental validation.
