My Story
01

From metallurgy
to membranes
to mountains.

I started as a metallurgist — learning to read steel microstructures, run XRD, characterize welds. Then I went to Saudi Arabia and spent a year at SABIC running 100+ SEM/EDS analyses on industrial components. That built the diagnostic instinct that now underpins everything I do.

In 2018 I co-founded a renewable energy startup in rural Sindh, Pakistan. We deployed 15+ off-grid solar and battery systems for communities with no grid access. That experience — the gap between what the technology could do and what people could actually afford and maintain — is why I pivoted into materials research for clean energy systems.

"The limiting factor was always energy storage: too expensive, not durable enough, not reliable enough for the conditions."

I won an Open Door International Scholarship (top 5% globally) to pursue an MSc at NUST MISIS in Moscow. My thesis is on engineering polymer electrolyte membranes for hydrogen fuel cells — fabricating catalyst-coated membranes via electrospinning, tracking degradation with EIS and SEM/EDS across 100+ thermal cycles. A journal paper is under review.

Alongside the thesis, I interned at Skoltech's Center for Digital Engineering, applying Python and MATLAB to signal processing problems in railway infrastructure monitoring. Different domain, same instinct: measure carefully, interpret honestly, improve systematically.
